Project 3 - Draft

The regression problem this project aims to solve is predicting sales volume and reorder quantities using the "Grocery Inventory and Sales Dataset" sourced from Kaggle. The dataset contains information on grocery products, including product categories, supplier details, stock levels, pricing, and sales performance metrics. By analyzing this dataset, we can gain insights into inventory and sales dynamics within the grocery sector.

Predicting sales volume and reorder quantities is a powerful tool for optimizing inventory management and supply chain efficiency. Challenges such as stockouts, overstocking, and food waste, can impact operational costs and customer satisfaction. By building a predictive model, this project aims to assist grocery stores in forecasting product demand more accurately leading to better inventory control and reducing waste. However, there are still some potential risks, such as over-reliance on the model, which might fail to account for unexpected demand surges during seasonal events or holidays.

The "Grocery Inventory and Sales Dataset" includes data on 990 products across various categories like Grains & Pulses, Beverages, and Fruits & Vegetables. Key features of the dataset include:

  • Reorder_Level: The stock level at which new stock should be ordered.
  • Reorder_Quantity: The quantity of product to order when the stock reaches the reorder level.
  • Sales_Volume: The total number of units sold.
  • Inventory_Turnover_Rate: The rate at which the product sells and is replenished.

What is Regression?

Before we get started, let discuss first what is regression and how does it apply to our problem statement? Regression is a statistical method used to predict a dependent variable based on one or more independent variables. In the context of this project, regression allows us to analyze the relationship between features like stock quantity, reorder level, and unit price (independent variables) and the sales volume or reorder quantity (dependent variables). Specifically, linear regression is one of the simplest forms of regression, where the relationship between the variables is modeled as a straight line.

**maybe math

Linear regression works by minimizing the error between the actual values and the predicted values through a process called Ordinary Least Squares (OLS). It calculates the optimal values for the coefficients, outputing the line of best fit. In this project, using regression to predict sales volume or reorder quantities can help grocery stores identify trends and optimize inventory management, reducing waste and improving efficiency.
